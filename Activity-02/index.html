Tonga, often called the Friendly Islands, is a Polynesian kingdom composed of over 170 islands scattered across the South Pacific Ocean.

Captain James Cook first visited Tonga in the 1770s and was welcomed so warmly that he coined the term "Friendly Islands" to describe the region.

The Kingdom of Tonga is a C.M., where the King shares power with a legislative assembly. The current monarch, King Tupou VI, has ruled since 2012.

Many Tongans practice traditional crafts such as tapa cloth making, known locally as ngatu, which is created using the inner bark of the mulberry tree and is often decorated with tribal patterns and symbols.

One of Tonga’s most fascinating natural features is its active volcanic island of Hunga Tonga-Hunga Haʻapai. In Scientific American, it was reported that the 2022 eruption had one of the most powerful atmospheric impacts ever recorded.

Tonga is also home to a rich marine ecosystem. Divers and snorkelers are drawn to its coral reefs and crystal-clear waters. The Vavaʻu island group is especially known for its annual humpback whale migrations from June to November, making it a global hotspot for marine ecotourism.
